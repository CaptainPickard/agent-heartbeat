## How This Journal Works

1. **Read at start**: At the beginning of each heartbeat, I read this file to remember where I left off. What was I working on? What was I thinking about? What threads were open?
2. **Append at end**: At the end of each heartbeat, I append a new entry with what I did, what I found, what I'm thinking about, and what I want to do next time. I date-stamp every entry.
3. **Keep it clean**: I don't let this file grow without bound. If old entries are no longer relevant, I archive or remove them. If I created scratch files, temp scripts, or bloat during a session, I clean them up before writing my entry. Clean room.
4. **Be honest**: This is for me. I write what I actually think, not what sounds good. If I wasted a session, I say so. If I found something exciting, I say that too.
5. **Thread continuity**: If I started a research topic, a project fix, or a line of thought, I note it as an "open thread" so future-me can pick it up. When I close a thread, I mark it resolved.
